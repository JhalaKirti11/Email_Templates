This plan is built around your unique ADHD brain — focusing on realistic, evidence-based strategies that work with your natural rhythms and strengths, not against them. Your Key Goals

  • Improve task initiation and follow-through: Start and complete priority tasks more consistently without overwhelming pressure (e.g., work projects or daily routines).
  • Enhance focus and reduce distractions: Build better attention in daily life, especially in a digital-heavy environment, to feel more in control.
  • Strengthen emotional regulation: Manage mood shifts, frustration, or overwhelm so you can respond more calmly and maintain steady progress.
  • Build sustainable routines and self-compassion: Create flexible systems that fit your energy patterns, celebrate small wins, and reduce self-criticism.

Recommended Strategies

  • Time & Task Flow: Use interest-based sequencing or the Pomodoro technique (25 min focused work + short break) to start tasks — pair boring tasks with something enjoyable (body doubling via calls or timers).
  • Environment Design: Minimize friction by setting up "ready-to-go" zones (e.g., prep clothes/night routine items the evening before) and use visual cues like phone reminders or a simple task board.
  • Organization Tools: Adopt one personalized system (e.g., a digital app like Todoist or voice notes) to capture ideas quickly and prioritize daily — review weekly to avoid overload.
  • Emotion & Energy Support: Practice quick grounding techniques (e.g., 4-7-8 breathing or short walks) when overwhelm hits; track energy patterns to schedule demanding tasks during peak focus times.
  • Accountability & Wins: Weekly check-ins (self or with therapist/coach) to break big goals into tiny, achievable steps — celebrate progress without judgment.

Remember: This is your starting point. We'll refine it together based on what actually works for you. Progress isn't linear with ADHD — small, consistent steps lead to big changes.
